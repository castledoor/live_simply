Mouse Defense

For those who plan to spend more than one night in the same camp, a mouse trap or two may be a boon. (Seems to take the critters a few nights to zero in.) I dont like killing them, however I see no alternative since they can cause alot of damage. A trap is better than staying up all night trying in vail to swat them. Many things work for bait: peanut butter, sunflower seeds, nuts, popcorn, bread (moistened and molded on),apple, carrot, cheese, etc., but I find a kernel of dried field corn holds up best.

Tying your trap to something will prevent its being carried off by a mouse that gets caught without being killed: or by a predator taking the mouse, trap and all. Tying with an elsastic rubber strap may keep a mouse that's caught only by the tail of a leg from jerking loose. For a tie point I drill a hole in the trap at the corner on the end opposuite the bait, so as not to interfere with the mechanism.

If I dont know whether I'll be contending with mice or rats, I prefer to have mouse traps. THey are lighter, cheaper, and more sensitive. And although a mouse trap wont catch a rat, it will mortally wound it. One morning I found a mouse trap sprung with nothing in it. 20 ft down the path lay a dead rat bleeding from one ear. If the rat survives, having more brains than a mouse, it may learn not to come around again.

Julie Summers, Oregon, February (sometime between 1980-1989)
